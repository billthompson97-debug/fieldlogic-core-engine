# Operational Graph Architecture

## Purpose

The Operational Graph models the relationships between operational entities inside residential remodeling operations.

Traditional systems store disconnected records.

FieldLogic models relationships.

## Core relationship examples

- installers assigned to jobs
- callbacks caused by upstream failures
- QA events linked to labor phases
- material packages connected to scope risk
- schedule delays related to procurement events

## Why this matters

Once operational relationships are modeled:
- root-cause analysis improves
- hidden operational patterns emerge
- AI systems gain context awareness
- production optimization becomes possible
- benchmarking becomes significantly more accurate

## Strategic significance

The Operational Graph becomes the connective tissue between:
- telemetry
- lineage
- replay
- forecasting
- anomaly detection
- prediction engines

## Long-term objective

FieldLogic should eventually operate as a continuously evolving operational knowledge graph for residential remodeling.
